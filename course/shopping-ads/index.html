Shopping Ads vs Search Ads

Shopping Ads

  • Visual product images

  • Manual ad copy creation

What is Google Merchant Center?

The hub where you upload and manage all your product data for Google Shopping

  • Centralised platform for product inventory management
  • Validates product data against Google's requirements
  • Connects your product feed to Google Ads campaigns
  • Provides diagnostics and error reporting for feed issues
  • Required for all Shopping Ads campaigns

Feed Optimization Tips

Product Titles

  • Include brand, product type, colour, size, and key features
  • Front-load the most important information
  • Use natural language that customers search for

Product Images

  • Use high-resolution images (minimum 800x800px)
  • Clean white or neutral backgrounds work best
  • Show the product clearly, no watermarks or borders

Pricing Strategy

  • Keep prices competitive and up-to-date
  • Include sale prices when running promotions

How the Auction Works

No keywords required. Google automatically matches search queries to your product feed data

  • User searches for a product on Google
  • Google scans product feeds from Merchant Center accounts
  • Algorithm matches search intent with product titles, descriptions, and categories
  • Eligible products enter real-time auction based on bid and quality
  • Winning ads are displayed at the top of search results

Bidding Strategies for Shopping

Manual CPC

Set maximum cost-per-click bids manually for each product group. Best for experienced advertisers who want full control.

Target ROAS

Set a target return on ad spend (e.g., 400%). Google automatically adjusts bids to hit your revenue goal.

Maximize Conversion Value

Google automatically sets bids to get the highest total purchase value within your budget. Great for scaling.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Poor product feed quality: Incomplete titles, low-quality images, or missing GTINs will hurt performance
  • Missing GTIN data: Google requires Global Trade Item Numbers for most products. don't skip this
  • Ignoring negative keywords: Standard Shopping allows negatives. use them to filter irrelevant traffic
  • Not segmenting product groups: Bidding the same for all products wastes budget on low performers
  • Using low-quality images: Blurry, pixelated, or watermarked images tank your click-through rate
  • Neglecting mobile experience: Most Shopping Ads clicks come from mobile. ensure fast load times
  • Setting and forgetting: Shopping campaigns require ongoing optimisation to stay profitable
