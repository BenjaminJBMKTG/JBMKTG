📋 Keyword Strategy Notes

  • Focus on exact match for high-intent terms — "zero waste [product]", "plastic free [product]", "eco friendly [product]" all show strong purchase intent
  • EWG Verified is a unique differentiator — Include in campaigns to attract health-conscious, ingredient-focused shoppers
  • Gift-related keywords spike seasonally — Increase budgets for "gift set", "gift basket" campaigns during Q4, Mother's Day, Father's Day
  • Starter kits and bundles are gateway products — These typically have higher AOV and introduce multiple product categories at once
  • Laundry and dishwashing products have high search volume — These are repeat-purchase categories with strong commercial intent
  • Body bars and shampoo bars are trending — Growing category with less competition than traditional liquid products
  • Use negative keywords strategically — Exclude "DIY", "recipe", "how to make" to avoid informational traffic
  • Monitor brand + competitor terms — Add observation campaigns for competitor brands in the zero waste space
  • Geo-targeting considerations — Focus on urban areas and regions with high environmental awareness (West Coast, Northeast, college towns)
  • Mobile optimization is critical — 60%+ of eco-conscious shoppers research on mobile before purchasing

✅ Next Steps

  • Launch with exact and phrase match in high-priority keywords (weeks 1-2)
  • Monitor search term reports daily and add negative keywords to protect budget
  • Build out Performance Max campaigns with optimized product feed (week 2)
  • Test responsive search ads with 3-5 headline/description variations per ad group
  • Set up conversion tracking for purchases, add-to-cart, and email signups
  • Implement remarketing tags and build audience lists by category/behavior

  • A/B test landing pages focused on EWG verification and zero waste USPs
